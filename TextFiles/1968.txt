==Events==

===January===
[[File:Captain Franklin P. Eller during Tet Offensive Vietnam.jpg|thumb|125px|right| [[January 30]]: [[Tet Offensive|Tet]] begins.]]
* [[January 5th]] &ndash; ''[[Prague Spring]]'': [[Alexander Dubček]] is chosen as the leader of the Communist Party in [[Czechoslovakia]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Navazelskis|first=Inabhfghh|title=Alexander Dubcek|publisher=Chelsea House Publications|year=1990|isbn=1-55546-831-4}}</ref>
* [[January 8]] &ndash; British Prime Minister [[Harold Wilson]] endorses the ''[[I'm Backing Britain]]'' campaign for working an additional half-hour each day without pay.<ref name="Wilson after-dinner speech">{{cite news|first=John|last=Chartres|title=Wilson Joins 'I Back Britain'|newspaper=[[The Times]]|location=London|date=9 January 1968|page=1}}</ref>
* [[January 14]] &ndash; The [[1967 Green Bay Packers season|Green Bay Packers]] defeat the [[1967 Oakland Raiders season|Oakland Raiders]] by the score of 33-14 in [[Super Bowl II]] at the [[Miami Orange Bowl]].
* [[January 15]] &ndash; An [[1968 Belice earthquake|earthquake]] in Sicily kills 380 and injures around 1,000.<ref>{{cite news|title=Italy: The Day the Earth Shook|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,837724,00.html|newspaper=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|date=26 January 1968|accessdate=2 August 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://emidius.mi.ingv.it/CPTI99/CPTI_finestre.html|title=Catalogo Parametrico dei Terremoti Italiani}}</ref>
* [[January 17]] &ndash; [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] calls for the non-conversion of the U.S. dollar.
* [[January 21]]
** [[Vietnam War]] &ndash; [[Battle of Khe Sanh]]: One of the most publicized and controversial battles of the war begins, ending on [[April 8]].
** A U.S. [[B-52 Stratofortress]] [[1968 Thule Air Base B-52 crash|crashes in Greenland]], discharging 4 [[nuclear bomb]]s.
* [[January 22]] &ndash; ''[[Rowan & Martin's Laugh-In]]'' debuts on [[NBC]].
* [[January 23]] &ndash; [[North Korea]] seizes the ''[[USS Pueblo (AGER-2)|USS Pueblo]]'', claiming the ship violated its territorial waters while spying.
* [[January 25]] &ndash; The [[Israel]]i submarine ''[[INS Dakar]]'' sinks in the Mediterranean Sea, killing 69.
[[File:USS Pueblo (AGER-2).jpg|thumb|125px|right| [[January 23]] ''[[USS Pueblo (AGER-2)|USS Pueblo]]'']]
* [[January 28]] &ndash; The [[France|French]] submarine ''[[French submarine Minerve (S647)|Minerve]]'' sinks in the Mediterranean Sea, killing 52.
* [[January 30]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[Tet Offensive]] begins, as [[Viet Cong]] forces launch a series of surprise attacks across [[South Vietnam]].
* [[January 31]]
** [[Việt Cộng]] soldiers attack the [[US Embassy, Saigon]].
** [[Nauru]] president [[Hammer DeRoburt]] declares independence from Australia.

===February===
* [[February 1]]
** [[Vietnam War]]: A [[Viet Cong]] officer named [[Nguyễn Văn Lém]] is executed by [[Nguyễn Ngọc Loan]], a [[South Vietnam]]ese National Police Chief. The event is photographed by [[Eddie Adams (photographer)|Eddie Adams]]. The photo makes headlines around the world, eventually winning the [[1969 Pulitzer Prize]], and sways U.S. public opinion against the war.
** The [[Pennsylvania Railroad]] and the [[New York Central Railroad]] merge to form [[Penn Central]], the largest ever corporate [[Mergers and acquisitions|merger]] up to this date.
* [[February 6]]&ndash;[[February 18]] &ndash; The [[1968 Winter Olympics]] are held in [[Grenoble]], France.
* [[February 8]] &ndash; [[American civil rights movement]]: A [[Orangeburg massacre|civil rights protest]] staged at a white-only [[bowling alley]] in [[Orangeburg, South Carolina]] is broken up by highway patrolmen; 3 college students are killed.
* [[February 11]]
**  Border clashes take place between [[Israel]] and [[Jordan]].
** [[Madison Square Garden]] in New York City opens at its current location.
* [[February 12]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[Phong Nhị and Phong Nhất massacre]].
* [[February 13]] &ndash; Civil rights disturbances occur at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] and the [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]].
* [[February 17]] &ndash; Administrative reforms in [[Romania]] divide the country into 39 [[județ|counties]].
* [[February 19]] 
** The [[Florida Education Association]] (FEA) initiates a mass resignation of teachers to protest state funding of education. This is, in effect, the first statewide [[Florida statewide teachers' strike of 1968|teachers' strike]] in the United States.
** [[National Educational Television|NET]] televises the very first episode of ''[[Mister Rogers' Neighborhood]]''.
* [[February 24]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[Tet Offensive]] is halted; [[South Vietnam]] recaptures [[Huế]].
* [[February 25]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[Hà My massacre]].
* [[February 27]] &ndash; Ex-[[The Teenagers|Teenagers]] singer [[Frankie Lymon]] is found dead from a [[heroin]] [[overdose]] in [[Harlem]].

=== March ===

* [[March 7]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[First Battle of Saigon]] ends.
* [[March 8]] &ndash; The first student protests spark the [[1968 Polish political crisis]].
Vietnam War]]: [[Battle of Lima Site 85]], the largest single ground combat loss of United States Air Force members (12) during the then-secret war later known as the [[Laotian Civil War]].
* [[March 11]] &ndash; U.S. President [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] mandates that all computers purchased by the federal government support the [[ASCII]] [[character encoding]].<ref>[[Lyndon B. Johnson]] (March 11, 1968). [http://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/ws/index.php?pid=28724 Memorandum Approving the Adoption by the Federal Government of a Standard Code for Information Interchange]. * [[March 12]]
** [[Mauritius]] achieves independence from British rule.
** U.S. President [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] barely edges out antiwar candidate [[Eugene McCarthy]] in the [[New Hampshire Democratic primary]], a vote which highlights the deep divisions in the country, and the party, over [[Vietnam]].
* [[March 13]] &ndash; The first [[Rotaract]] club is chartered in [[North Charlotte]], North Carolina.
* [[March 14]] &ndash; [[Nerve gas]] leaks from the U.S. Army [[Dugway Proving Ground]] near [[Skull Valley (Utah)|Skull Valley, Utah]].
* [[March 15]] &ndash; British [[Foreign Secretary]] [[George Brown, Baron George-Brown|George Brown]] resigns.
* [[March 16]]
** [[Vietnam War]] &ndash; [[My Lai Massacre]]: American troops kill scores of civilians. The story will first become public in November 1969 and will help undermine public support for the U.S. efforts in Vietnam.
** U.S. Senator [[Robert F. Kennedy]] enters the race for the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] presidential nomination.
* [[March 17]] &ndash; A demonstration in London's [[Grosvenor Square]] against U.S. involvement in the [[Vietnam War]] leads to violence; 91 people are injured, 200 demonstrators arrested.
* [[March 18]] &ndash; [[Gold standard]]: The [[United States Congress]] repeals the requirement for a [[gold]] reserve to back U.S. currency.
* [[March 19]]&ndash;[[March 23]] &ndash; [[Afrocentrism]], [[Black Power]], [[Vietnam War]]: Students at [[Howard University]] in Washington, D.C., signal a new era of militant student activism on college campuses in the U.S. Students stage rallies, protests and a 5-day sit-in, laying siege to the administration building, shutting down the university in protest over its [[ROTC]] program and the Vietnam War, and demanding a more Afrocentric curriculum.
* [[March 21]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: In ongoing campus unrest, [[Howard University]] students protesting the Vietnam War, the [[ROTC]] program on campus and the draft, confront Gen. [[Lewis Blaine Hershey|Lewis Hershey]], then head of the U.S. [[Selective Service System]], and as he attempts to deliver an address, shout him down with cries of "America is the Black man's battleground!"
* [[March 22]] &ndash; [[Daniel Cohn-Bendit]] ("Danny the Red") and 7 other students occupy the administrative offices of the [[University of Nanterre]], setting in motion a chain of events that lead France to the brink of revolution in May.
* [[March 24]] &ndash; [[Aer Lingus Flight 712]] crashes en route from [[Cork Airport|Cork]] to London near [[Tuskar Rock, Ireland|Tuskar Rock, Wexford]], killing 61 passengers and crew.
* [[March 26]] &ndash; [[Joan Baez]] marries activist [[David Harris (protester)|David Harris]] in New York.
* [[March 28]] &ndash; [[Brazil]]ian high school student [[Edson Luís de Lima Souto]] is shot by the police in a protest for cheaper meals at a restaurant for low-income students. The aftermath of his death is one of the first major events against the military dictatorship.
* [[March 31]] &ndash; U.S. President [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] announces he will not seek re-election.

=== April ===
* [[April 2]]
** Bombs explode at midnight in two department stores in [[Frankfurt-am-Main]]; [[Andreas Baader]] and [[Gudrun Ensslin]] are later arrested and sentenced for [[arson]].
** The film  ''[[2001: A Space Odyssey (film)|2001: A Space Odyssey]]'' premieres in Washington, D.C.
* [[April 3]] &ndash; The American movie ''[[Planet of the Apes (1968 film)|Planet of the Apes]]'' is released in theaters.
* [[April 4]]
** [[Martin Luther King, Jr.]] is [[Assassination of Martin Luther King, Jr.|shot dead]] at the [[Lorraine Motel]] in [[Memphis, Tennessee]]. [[King assassination riots|Riots erupt]] in major American cities, lasting for several days afterwards.
** [[Apollo program]]: Apollo-Saturn mission 502 (''[[Apollo 6]]'') is launched, as the second and last unmanned test-flight of the [[Saturn V]] launch vehicle.
* [[April 6]]
** ''[[La, la, la]]'' by [[Massiel]] (music and lyrics by Manuel de la Calva and Ramón Arcusa) wins the [[Eurovision Song Contest 1968]] for Spain, at the [[Royal Albert Hall]] in London.
** A shootout between [[Black Panthers]] and [[Oakland, California|Oakland]] police results in several arrests and deaths, including 16-year-old Panther [[Bobby Hutton]].
** A [[Richmond, Indiana explosion|double explosion]] in downtown [[Richmond, Indiana]] kills 41 and injures 150.
* [[April 7]] &ndash; Racing driver [[Jim Clark]] is killed in a [[Formula 2]] race at [[Hockenheimring|Hockenheim]].
* [[April 8]] &ndash; The [[Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs]] (under [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]) (BNDD) is created.
* [[April 10]] &ndash; The ferry {{ship|TEV|Wahine}} strikes a [[reef]] at the mouth of [[Wellington Harbour]], New Zealand, with the loss of 53 lives, in Cyclone Giselle, which created the windiest conditions ever recorded in New Zealand.
* [[April 11]]
** [[Josef Bachmann]] tries to assassinate [[Rudi Dutschke]], leader of the left-wing movement ([[Ausserparlamentarische Opposition|APO]]) in Germany, and tries to commit suicide afterwards, failing in both, although Dutschke dies of his brain injuries 11 years later.
** German left-wing students blockade the [[Axel Springer AG|Springer Press]] HQ in Berlin and many are arrested (one of them [[Ulrike Meinhof]]).
** U.S. President [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] signs the [[Civil Rights Act of 1968]].
** [[MGM]]'s classic film ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]'' makes its [[NBC]] debut after being telecast on [[CBS]] since 1956. It will remain on NBC for the next 8 years.
* [[April 20]]
** English politician [[Enoch Powell]] makes his controversial [[Rivers of Blood speech]].
* [[April 23]]
** President [[Mobutu]] releases captured [[mercenary|mercenaries]] in the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congo]].
** Surgeons at the Hôpital de la Pitié, Paris, perform Europe's first heart transplant, on Clovis Roblain.
** The [[United Methodist Church]] is created by the union of the former Methodist and Evangelical United Brethren churches.
* [[April 23]]&ndash;[[April 30]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: Student protesters at [[Columbia University]] in New York City take over administration buildings and shut down the university (see main article [[Columbia University protests of 1968]]).
* [[April 26]] &ndash; The [[nuclear weapon]] "Boxcar" is [[nuclear weapons testing|tested]] at the [[Nevada Test Site]] in the biggest detonation of [[Operation Crosstie]].
* [[April 29]] &ndash; The musical ''[[Hair (musical)|Hair]]'' officially opens on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]].

=== May ===
* [[May 2]] &ndash; The [[Israel Broadcasting Authority]] commences television broadcasts.
* [[May 3]] &ndash; [[Braniff Flight 352]] crashes near [[Dawson, Texas]], killing all 85 persons on board.
* [[May 13]] &ndash; [[May 1968 protests in France|Paris student riots]]: One million march through the streets of Paris.
* [[May 13]] &ndash; [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] wins the  [[1967–68 Football League First Division]] by 2 clear points, over club rivals Manchester United
* [[May 14]] &ndash; [[The Beatles]] announce the creation of [[Apple Records]] in a New York press conference.
* [[May 15]] &ndash; An outbreak of severe thunderstorms produces [[May 1968 tornado outbreak|tornadoes]], causing massive damage and heavy casualties in [[Charles City, Iowa]], [[Oelwein, Iowa]], and [[Jonesboro, Arkansas]].
* [[May 16]] &ndash; [[Ronan Point]], a 23 floor tower block in Canning Town, east London, partially collapses after a gas explosion, killing 5.
* [[May 17]] &ndash; The [[Catonsville Nine]] enter the [[Selective Service]] offices in [[Catonsville, Maryland]], take dozens of selective service draft records, and burn them with [[napalm]] as a protest against the [[Vietnam War]].
* May 18 &ndash; West Bromwich Albion win the Football Association Cup, defeating Everton 1-0 after extra time. The winning goal was scored by Jeff Astle.
* [[May 19]]
** [[Italian general election, 1968|A general election]] is held in Italy.
** Nigerian forces capture [[Port Harcourt]] and form a ring around the [[Nigerian Civil War|Biafrans]]. This contributes to a humanitarian disaster as the surrounded population already suffers from hunger and starvation.
* [[May 22]] &ndash; The U.S. nuclear-powered [[submarine]] [[USS Scorpion (SSN-589)|''Scorpion'']] sinks with 99 men aboard, 400 miles southwest of the [[Azores]].
* [[May 29]] &ndash; [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] wins the [[European Champion Clubs' Cup|European Cup Final]], becoming the first English team to do so.
* [[May 30]] &ndash; [[Bobby Unser]] wins the [[Indianapolis 500]].

=== June ===
*[[June 1]] &ndash; [[Helen Keller]] dies at the age of 87 years.
* [[June 3]] &ndash; [[Radical feminism|Radical feminist]] [[Valerie Solanas]] shoots [[Andy Warhol]] as he enters his studio, wounding him.
* [[June 4]] &ndash; The [[S&P 500|Standard & Poor's 500]] index closes above 100 for the first time, at 100.38.
* [[June 4]] &ndash; [[Pierre Elliott Trudeau]] becomes the 15th Prime Minister of Canada.<ref>
"Pierre Elliott Trudeau." Prime Minister of Canada. August 26, 2013. Accessed April 8, 2015.</ref>
* [[June 5]] &ndash; U.S. presidential candidate [[Robert F. Kennedy]] is [[Assassination of Robert F. Kennedy|shot]] at the [[Ambassador Hotel (Los Angeles)|Ambassador Hotel]] in Los Angeles by [[Sirhan Sirhan]]. Kennedy dies from his injuries the next day.
* [[June 8]] &ndash; [[James Earl Ray]] is arrested for the assassination of [[Martin Luther King, Jr.]].
* [[June 10]] &ndash; [[Italy national football team|Italy]] beats [[Yugoslavia national football team|Yugoslavia]] 2–0 in a replay to win the [[UEFA Euro 1968|1968 European Championship]]. The original final on [[June 8]] ended 1–1.
* [[June 12]] &ndash; The film ''[[Rosemary's Baby (film)|Rosemary's Baby]]'' premieres in the U.S.
* [[June 17]] &ndash; The [[Malayan Communist Party]] launches a second insurgency and the [[Second Malayan Emergency|state of emergency]] is again imposed in [[Malaysia]].
* [[June 20]] &ndash; [[Austin Currie]], Member of Parliament at [[Parliament Buildings (Northern Ireland)|Stormont]] in [[Northern Ireland]], along with others, squats a house in [[Caledon, County Tyrone|Caledon]] to protest discrimination in housing allocations.
* [[June 23]] &ndash; A [[Superclásico#Puerta 12 tragedy|football stampede]] in [[Buenos Aires]] leaves 74 dead and 150 injured.
* [[June 24]] &ndash; Giorgio Rosa declares the independence of his [[Republic of Rose Island]], an artificial island off [[Rimini]], Italy. Italian troops demolish it not long after.
* [[June 26]] &ndash; The [[Bonin Islands]] are returned to Japan after 23 years of occupation by the United States Navy.
* [[June 30]] &ndash; The [[Lockheed C-5 Galaxy]] heavy [[military transport aircraft]] first flies in the U.S. This model will still be in service 40 years later.

===July===
* [[July 1]]
** The [[Central Intelligence Agency]]'s [[Phoenix Program]] is officially established.
** The [[Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty]] opens for signature.
* [[July 4]] &ndash; Yachtsman [[Alec Rose]], 59, receives a hero's welcome as he sails into [[Portsmouth]], England after his 354-day round-the-world trip.
* [[July 15]]
** &ndash; The [[soap opera]] ''[[One Life to Live]]'' premieres on [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]].
* [[July 17]] &ndash; [[Saddam Hussein]] becomes Vice Chairman of the Revolutionary Council in [[Iraq]] after a ''[[coup d'état]]''.
* [[July 18]] &ndash; The semiconductor company [[Intel]] is founded.
* [[July 20]] &ndash; The first International [[Special Olympics]] Summer Games are held at [[Soldier Field]] in Chicago, Ill, with about 1,000 athletes with intellectual disabilities. 
* [[July 23]]&ndash;[[July 28]] &ndash; Black militants led by Fred (Ahmed) Evans engage in a fierce gunfight with police in the [[Glenville Shootout]] of [[Cleveland, Ohio]], in the United States.
* [[July 25]] &ndash; [[Pope Paul VI]] publishes the [[encyclical]] entitled ''[[Humanae vitae]]'', condemning [[birth control]].
* [[July 26]] &ndash; Vietnam War: [[South Vietnam]]ese opposition leader [[Trương Đình Dzu]] is sentenced to 5 years [[hard labor]], for advocating the formation of a [[coalition government]] as a way to move toward an end to the [[war]].
* [[July 29]] &ndash; [[Arenal Volcano]] erupts in [[Costa Rica]] for the first time in centuries.
* [[July 30]] &ndash; [[Thames Television]] starts transmission in London.

===August===
* [[August 5]]&ndash;[[August 8]] &ndash; The [[Republican National Convention]] in [[Miami Beach, Florida]] nominates [[Richard Nixon]] for U.S. President and [[Spiro Agnew]] for Vice President.
* [[August 11]] &ndash; The last steam passenger train service runs in Britain. A selection of [[British Railways]] steam [[locomotive]]s make the 120-mile journey from [[Liverpool]] to [[Carlisle, Cumbria|Carlisle]] and return to Liverpool &ndash; the journey is known as the [[Fifteen Guinea Special]].
* [[August 18]] &ndash; Two charter buses are pushed into the [[Hida River]] on National Highway Route 41 in Japan, in an accident caused by heavy rain; 104 are killed.
* [[August 20]]&ndash;[[August 21]] &ndash; The ''[[Prague Spring]]'' of political liberalization ends, as 750,000 [[Warsaw Pact]] troops and 6,500 [[tank]]s with 800 planes [[Warsaw Pact invasion of Czechoslovakia|invade]] [[Czechoslovakia]]. It is dated as the biggest operation in Europe since WWII ended.
* [[August 21]] &ndash; The [[Medal of Honor]] is posthumously awarded to [[James Anderson, Jr.]]&mdash;he is the first black [[U.S. Marine]] to be awarded the Medal of Honor.
* [[August 24]] &ndash; France explodes its first hydrogen bomb.
* [[August 22]]&ndash;[[August 30]] &ndash; Police clash with anti-war protesters in Chicago, [[Illinois]], outside the [[1968 Democratic National Convention]], which nominates [[Hubert Humphrey]] for U.S. President, and [[Edmund Muskie]] for Vice President. The riots and subsequent trials were an essential part of the activism of the [[Youth International Party]].
* [[August 28]] &ndash; [[John Gordon Mein]], US Ambassador to Guatemala, is assassinated on the streets of Guatemala City. First US Ambassador assassinated in the line of duty.
* [[August 29]] &ndash; [[Harald V of Norway|Crown Prince Harald]] of Norway marries [[Sonja Haraldsen]], the commoner he has dated for 9 years, in [[Oslo]].

===September===
* [[September 6]]
** [[Swaziland]] becomes independent.
** 150 women (members of [[New York Radical Women]]) arrive in [[Atlantic City, New Jersey]] to [[No More Miss America|protest against]] the [[Miss America]] Pageant, as exploitative of women. Led by activist and author [[Robin Morgan]], it is one of the first large demonstrations of [[Second Wave Feminism]] as [[Women's Liberation]] begins to gather much media attention.
* [[September 11]]
** The [[International Association of Classification Societies]] (IACS) is founded.
** French General [[René Cogny]] and 94 others die in an [[Air France]] [[Sud Aviation Caravelle|Caravelle]] jetliner crash near [[Nice]] in the [[Mediterranean]].
* [[September 13]]
** [[Albania]] officially retreats from the Warsaw Pact upon the Soviet Union-led Warsaw Pact invasion of Czechoslovakia, having already ceased to participate actively in Pact activity since [[1962]].
** US Army Maj. Gen. [[Keith L. Ware]], WWII Medal of Honor recipient, is killed when his helicopter is shot down in [[Vietnam War|Vietnam]]. He is posthumously awarded the [[Distinguished Service Cross (United States)|Distinguished Service Cross]].
** Agreement for [[Mergers and acquisitions|merger]] between the [[General Electric Company plc|General Electric Company]] and [[English Electric]], the largest industrial merger in the UK up to this date.
* [[September 14]] &ndash; [[Detroit Tigers|Detroit Tiger]] [[Denny McLain]] becomes the first baseball pitcher to win 30 games in a season since 1934. He remains the last to accomplish the feat.
* [[September 17]] &ndash; The [[D'Oliveira affair]]: The [[Marylebone Cricket Club]] tour of South Africa is cancelled when the South Africans refuse to accept the presence of [[Basil D'Oliveira]], a [[Cape Coloured]], in the side.
* [[September 20]] &ndash; ''[[Hawaii Five-O]]'' debuts on [[CBS]], and eventually becomes the longest-running crime show in television history, until ''[[Law & Order]]'' overtakes it in 2003.
* [[September 21]] &ndash; The Soviet's [[Zond 5]] unmanned lunar flyby mission returns to earth, with its first of a kind biological payload intact.
* [[September 23]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[Tet Offensive]] comes to an end in [[South Vietnam]].
* [[September 24]] &ndash; ''[[60 Minutes]]'' debuts on [[CBS]] and is still on the air as of [[2016]].
* [[September 27]] &ndash; [[Marcelo Caetano]] becomes [[prime minister]] of Portugal.
* [[September 29]] &ndash; A [[referendum]] in Greece gives more power to the military junta.
* [[September 30]] &ndash; At Paine Field, near Everett, Washington in the United States, [[Boeing]] officially rolls out its new [[Boeing 747|747]] for the media and the public.

===October===
* [[October 2]] &ndash; [[Tlatelolco massacre]]: A student demonstration ends in bloodbath at La Plaza de las Tres Culturas in [[Tlatelolco (Mexico City)|Tlatelolco]], Mexico City, Mexico, 10 days before the inauguration of the [[1968 Summer Olympics]].
* [[October 3]] &ndash; In [[Peru]], [[Juan Velasco Alvarado]] takes power in a revolution.
* [[October 5]] &ndash; [[Royal Ulster Constabulary|Police]] baton [[Northern Ireland Civil Rights Association|civil rights]] demonstrators in [[Derry]], [[Northern Ireland]], marking the beginning of [[The Troubles]].
* [[October 8]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]] &ndash; [[Operation Sealords]]: United States and [[South Vietnam]]ese forces launch a new operation in the [[Mekong Delta]].
* [[October 11]]
** [[Apollo program]]: [[NASA]] launches ''[[Apollo 7]]'', the first manned Apollo mission ([[Wally Schirra]], [[Donn Eisele]], [[Walter Cunningham]]). Mission goals include the first live [[television]] broadcast from [[orbit]] and testing the [[lunar module]] docking maneuver.
** In [[Panama]], a military ''coup d'état'', led by Col. Boris Martinez and Col. [[Omar Torrijos]], overthrows the democratically elected (but highly controversial) government of President [[Arnulfo Arias]]. Within a year, Torrijos ousts Martinez and takes charge as de facto [[Head of Government]] in Panama.
* [[October 12]]&ndash;[[October 27]] &ndash; The [[Games of the XIX Olympiad]] are held in Mexico City, Mexico.
* [[October 12]] &ndash; [[Equatorial Guinea]] receives its independence from Spain.
* [[October 14]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[United States Department of Defense]] announces that the [[United States Army]] and [[United States Marines]] will send about 24,000 troops back to [[Vietnam]] for involuntary second tours.
* [[October 15]] &ndash; [[Led Zeppelin]] makes their first live performance, at [[Surrey University]] in England<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockhall.com/inductees/led-zeppelin |title=Rock & Roll Hall of Fame |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/20150717033456/https://www.rockhall.com/inductees/led-zeppelin/ |archivedate=July 17, 2015 }}</ref>
* [[October 16]]
** In Mexico City, African-American athletes [[Tommie Smith]] and [[John Carlos]] raise their arms in a [[1968 Olympics Black Power salute|black power salute]] after winning, respectively, the gold and bronze medals in the Olympic men's 200 metres.
** [[Kingston, Jamaica]] is rocked by the [[Rodney Riots]], provoked by the banning of [[Walter Rodney]] from the country.
* [[October 20]] &ndash; Former U.S. First Lady [[Jacqueline Kennedy]] marries Greek shipping tycoon [[Aristotle Onassis]] on the Greek island of [[Skorpios]].
* [[October 22]] &ndash; The [[Gun Control Act of 1968]] is enacted.
* [[October 31]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: Citing progress in the Paris peace talks, U.S. President [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] announces to the nation that he has ordered a complete cessation of "all air, naval, and artillery bombardment of [[North Vietnam]]" effective [[November 1]].

===November===
* [[November 5]]
** [[U.S. presidential election, 1968]]: [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] challenger [[Richard Nixon]] defeats the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] candidate, Vice President [[Hubert Humphrey]], and [[American Independent Party]] candidate [[George Wallace|George C. Wallace]].
** [[Luis A. Ferré]], of the newly formed [[New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico|New Progressive Party]] is elected [[Governor of Puerto Rico]], by beating incumbent governor [[Roberto Sánchez Vilella]] of the [[People's Party (Puerto Rico)|People's Party]], [[Luis Negrón López]] of the [[Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico|Popular Democratic Party]] and Antonio J. Gonzalez of the [[Puerto Rican Independence Party]], he also becomes the first "statehooder" governor of the Island.
* [[November 11]]
** [[Vietnam War]]: [[Operation Commando Hunt]] is initiated to interdict men and supplies on the [[Ho Chi Minh trail]], through [[Laos]] into [[South Vietnam]]. By the end of the operation, 3 million tons of bombs are dropped on Laos, slowing but not seriously disrupting trail operations.
** A second [[republic]] is declared in the [[Maldives]].
* [[November 14]] &ndash; [[Yale University]] announces it is going to admit women.
* [[November 17]] &ndash; The [[Heidi Game]]: [[NBC]] cuts off the final 1:05 of an [[Oakland Raiders]]–[[New York Jets]] football game to broadcast the pre-scheduled ''[[Heidi (1968 film)|Heidi]]''. Fans are unable to see Oakland (which had been trailing 32–29) score 2 late touchdowns to win 43–32; as a result, thousands of outraged football fans flood the NBC switchboards to protest.
* [[November 19]] &ndash; In [[Mali]], President [[Modibo Keïta]]'s regime is overthrown in a bloodless [[military coup]] led by [[Moussa Traoré]].<ref>{{citation|url=http://lcweb2.loc.gov/frd/cs/profiles/Mali.pdf|title=Mali country profile|publisher=[[Library of Congress]] [[Federal Research Division]]|location=Washington, D.C. |page=3 |date=January 2005}}</ref>

===December===
* [[December 3]] &ndash; If I Can Dream marks the concert return of [[Elvis Presley]].
* [[December 6]] &ndash; [[The Rolling Stones]] release ''[[Beggars Banquet]]'', which contains the classic song "[[Sympathy for the Devil]]."
* [[December 9]] &ndash; [[Douglas Engelbart]] publicly demonstrates his pioneering [[hypertext]] system, [[NLS (computer system)|NLS]], in [[San Francisco]], together with the [[computer mouse]], at what becomes retrospectively known as "[[The Mother of All Demos]]".
* [[December 10]] &ndash; Japan's biggest heist, the never-solved "[[300 million yen robbery]]", occurs in Tokyo.
* [[December 11]] &ndash; The film ''[[Oliver! (film)|Oliver!]]'', based on the hit London and [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] musical, opens in the U.S. after being released first in England. It goes on to win the [[Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]].
** ''[[The Rolling Stones Rock and Roll Circus]]'' is also filmed on this date, but not released until [[1996]].
* [[December 13]] &ndash; Prompted by growing unrest and proliferation of pro-communist terrorist actions, [[Brazil]]ian [[President of Brazil|president]] [[Artur da Costa e Silva]] enacts the so-called [[AI-5]], the fifth of a series of non-constitutional emergency decrees that helped stabilize the country after the turmoils of the early 1960s.
* [[December 17]] &ndash; 11-year-old [[Mary Bell]] is found guilty of murdering two small boys and sentenced to life in [[Detention (imprisonment)|Detention]], but is later released from prison in [[1980]] and granted anonymity.
* [[December 20]] &ndash; The [[Zodiac Killer]] is believed to have shot Betty Lou Jensen and David Faraday on Lake Herman Road, [[Benicia]], [[San Francisco Bay]], [[California]].
* [[December 22]]
** [[David Eisenhower]], grandson of former U.S. President [[Dwight D. Eisenhower]], marries [[Julie Nixon]], the daughter of U.S. President-elect [[Richard Nixon]].
** [[Mao Zedong]] advocates that educated youth in urban China be re-educated in the country. It marks the start of the [[Down to the Countryside Movement|"Up to the mountains and down to the villages" movement]].
* [[December 24]] &ndash; [[Apollo program]]: U.S. spacecraft ''[[Apollo 8]]'' enters orbit around the [[Moon]]. Astronauts [[Frank Borman]], [[Jim Lovell]] and [[William A. Anders]] become the first humans to see the [[Far side (Moon)|far side]] of the [[Moon]] and planet [[Earth]] as a whole. The crew also [[Apollo 8 Genesis reading|reads from Genesis]].
* [[December 26]] &ndash; [[Led Zeppelin]] make their American debut in [[Denver, CO]].
* [[December 28]] &ndash; Israeli forces launch [[1968 Israeli raid on Lebanon|an attack on Beirut airport]], destroying more than a dozen aircraft.<!--Some references say 13, others 14.-->
