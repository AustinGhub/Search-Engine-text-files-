== Events ==

=== January ===
* [[January 1]] &ndash; New [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] franchises, [[ITV Central|Central]], [[ITV Meridian|TVS]] and [[ITV South West|TSW]], are launched.
* [[January 7]] &ndash; The [[Commodore 64]] [[8-bit]] [[home computer]] is launched by [[Commodore International]] in [[Las Vegas Valley|Las Vegas]]<ref>{{cite web|title=This Day in Geek History: January 7|url=http://thegreatgeekmanual.com/blog/this-day-in-geek-history-january-7|work=The Great Geek Manual|accessdate=2013-03-25}}</ref> (released in August); it becomes the all-time best-selling single personal computer model.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pagetable.com/?p=547|title=How many Commodore 64 computers were sold?|work=pagetable|accessdate=2013-03-25}}</ref>
* [[January 8]] &ndash; [[AT&T Corporation]] agrees to break up and divest itself of 22 subdivisions.<ref name="Friday, 8 January 1982: What the world looked like - Historical Events">{{cite web|title=Friday, 8 January 1982: What the world looked like - Historical Events|url=http://takemeback.to/08-January-1982#.VSwZAJNy-og|website=Takemeback.to|accessdate=13 April 2015|ref=Friday, 8 January 1982: What the world looked like - Historical Events}}</ref>
* [[January 11]] &ndash; [[Mark Thatcher]], son of British Prime Minister [[Margaret Thatcher]], disappears in the [[Sahara]] during the [[Dakar Rally]]; he is rescued [[January 14]].
* [[January 11]] &ndash; [[January 17]] &ndash; A brutal cold snap sends temperatures to all-time record lows in dozens of cities throughout the [[Midwestern United States]].
* [[January 13]] &ndash; Shortly after takeoff, [[Air Florida Flight 90]] crashes into Washington, D.C.'s 14th Street Bridge and falls into the [[Potomac River]], killing 78. On the same day, a [[Washington Metro]] train derails to the north, killing 3 (the system's first fatal accident).
* [[January 17]] &ndash; [[Cold Sunday]] sweeps over the [[northern United States]].
* [[January 18]] &ndash; [[1982 Thunderbirds Indian Springs Diamond Crash]]: Four [[Northrop T-38]] aircraft of the [[United States Air Force Thunderbirds]] Demonstration Squadron crash at [[Indian Springs Air Force Auxiliary Field]], Nevada, killing all 4 pilots.
* [[January 26]]
** [[Mauno Koivisto]] is elected [[President of Finland]].
** [[Unemployment]] in the United Kingdom increases by 129,918 to 3,070,621, a post-war record number.
* [[January 27]] &ndash; The [[Garret FitzGerald]] government of the [[Republic of Ireland]] is defeated 82–81 on its budget; the [[Members of the 22nd Dáil|22nd Dáil Éireann]] is dissolved.
* [[January 28]] &ndash; United States Army Brigadier General [[James L. Dozier]] is rescued by the Italian anti-[[terrorism]] [[Nucleo Operativo Centrale di Sicurezza]] (NOCS) force after being held captive for 42 days by the [[Red Brigades]].
* [[January 30]] &ndash; The first [[computer virus]], the [[Elk Cloner]], written by 15-year old [[Rich Skrenta]], is found.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://searchsecurity.techtarget.com/sDefinition/0,,sid14_gci989616,00.html|title=Elk Cloner|work=SearchSecurity |accessdate=2012-01-28 |date=June 2004}}</ref> It infects [[Apple II]] computers via [[floppy disk]].

=== February ===
* [[February 1]] &ndash; [[Senegal]] and [[The Gambia|Gambia]] form a loose [[Senegambia Confederation]].
* [[February 2]] &ndash; The [[Hama massacre]] begins in [[Syria]].
* [[February 3]] &ndash; [[Syria]]n president [[Hafez al-Assad]] orders the army to purge the city of [[Harran]] of the [[Muslim Brotherhood]].
* [[February 5]] &ndash; London-based [[Laker Airways]] collapses, leaving 6,000 stranded passengers and debts of $270 million.
* [[February 7]] &ndash; [[Iraq]]i club [[Al-Shorta]] win the [[1982 Arab Club Champions Cup]] with a 4–2 aggregate win over [[Nejmeh SC|Al-Nejmeh]] in the final.
* [[February 9]] &ndash; [[Japan Airlines Flight 350]] crashes in [[Tokyo Bay]] due to [[thrust reversal]] on approach to [[Tokyo International Airport]], killing 24 among the 174 people on board.
* [[February 14]] &ndash; Jae Lee (CS3157 Founder) was born.
* [[February 15]] &ndash; The [[oil platform]] ''[[Ocean Ranger]]'' sinks during a storm off the coast of [[Newfoundland (island)|Newfoundland]], killing all 84 rig workers aboard.
* [[February 18]] &ndash; The [[Irish general election, February 1982|Republic of Ireland general election]] gives a boost to [[Fianna Fáil]].
* [[February 19]] &ndash; The [[DeLorean Motor Company]] Car Factory in [[Belfast]] is put into [[receivership]].
* [[February 24]] &ndash; In South Africa, 22 [[National Party (South Africa)|National Party]] MPs led by [[Andries Treurnicht]] vote for no confidence in [[P. W. Botha]].
* [[February 25]] &ndash; The [[European Court of Human Rights]] rules that teachers who cane, belt or tase children against the wishes of their parents are in breach of the Human Rights Convention.
* [[February 27]] &ndash; [[Atlanta murders of 1979–81]]: [[Wayne Williams]] is convicted of murdering 2 adult men and is sentenced to two consecutive [[Life imprisonment|life terms]].

=== March ===
* [[March 3]] &ndash; [[Elizabeth II]] opens the [[Barbican Centre]] in London.
* [[March 9]] &ndash; [[Charles Haughey]] becomes [[Taoiseach]] of the [[Republic of Ireland]].
* [[March 10]]
** The United States places an [[embargo]] on [[Libya]]n [[petroleum|oil]] imports, alleging Libyan [[State-sponsored terrorism|support for terrorist groups]].
** [[Syzygy (astronomy)|Syzygy]]: All 8 planets align on the same side of the Sun (see also ''[[The Jupiter Effect]]'').
* [[March 16]] &ndash; In [[Newport, Rhode Island]], [[Claus von Bülow]] is found guilty of the attempted murder of his wife.
* [[March 18]]
** An [[Argentina|Argentine]] scrap metal dealer raises the Argentine flag in [[South Georgia]].
** [[Mary Whitehouse]]'s private prosecution of ''[[The Romans in Britain]]'' collapses.
* [[March 26]] &ndash; A ground-breaking ceremony for the [[Vietnam Veterans Memorial]] is held in Washington, D.C.
* [[March 29]]
** [[Royal Assent]] is given to the [[Canada Act 1982]], setting the stage for the repatriation of the [[Canadian Constitution]] on [[April 17]].
** The [[54th Academy Awards]], hosted by [[Johnny Carson]], are held at the [[Dorothy Chandler Pavilion]] in [[Los Angeles]]. ''[[Chariots of Fire]]'' wins [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]] and 3 other [[Academy Awards]].

===April===
* [[April 2]] &ndash; The [[Falklands War]] begins: [[Argentina]] [[1982 invasion of the Falkland Islands|invades]] and [[Occupation of the Falkland Islands|occupies]] the [[Falkland Islands]].
* [[April 4]] &ndash; [[Mexico]]'s children's festival "Juguemos a Cantar" takes place and ends its first run on May 2 .
* [[April 6]] &ndash; A blizzard unprecedented in size for April dumps 1–2 feet of snow on the [[northeastern United States]], closing schools and businesses, snarling traffic, and canceling several major league baseball games.
* [[April 17]] &ndash; By Proclamation of the [[Monarchy of Canada|Queen of Canada]] on [[Parliament Hill]], Canada [[patriate]]s its [[constitution]], gaining full political independence from the United Kingdom; included is the country's first [[Entrenched clause|entrenched]] [[bill of rights]].
* [[April 23]] &ndash; [[Dennis Wardlow]], mayor of [[Key West, Florida]], declares the independent "[[Conch Republic]]" for a day.
* [[April 24]] &ndash; German singer Nicole wins the [[Eurovision Song Contest 1982]] for Germany, with the song "Ein Bisschen Frieden".
* [[April 25]] &ndash; [[Israel]] completes its withdrawal from the [[Sinai Peninsula]] in accordance with the [[Egypt–Israel Peace Treaty]] of [[1979]].
* [[April 26]] &ndash; [[Falklands War]]: British troops retake [[South Georgia]] during [[Operation Paraquet]].
* [[April 30]] &ndash; The [[Bijon Setu massacre]] takes place in India.

=== May ===
* [[May 1]] &ndash; A crowd of over 100,000 attends the first day of the [[1982 World's Fair]] in [[Knoxville, Tennessee]], which is kicked off with an address by President [[Ronald Reagan]]. Over 11 million people attend during its 6-month run.
* [[May 2]]
** [[Falklands War]]: The nuclear submarine {{HMS|Conqueror|S48|6}} sinks the Argentine cruiser [[ARA General Belgrano|''General Belgrano'']], killing 323 sailors. [[Operation Algeciras]], an attempt to destroy a [[Royal Navy]] warship in [[Gibraltar]], fails.
** [[The Weather Channel]] airs on [[cable television]] for the first time.
* [[May 4]] &ndash; [[Falklands War]]: {{HMS|Sheffield|D80|6}} is hit by an [[Exocet]] missile, and burns out of control; 20 sailors are killed. The ship sinks on [[May 10]].
* [[May 5]] &ndash; A [[Unabomber]] bomb explodes in the [[computer science]] department at [[Vanderbilt University]]; secretary Janet Smith is injured.
* [[May 8]] &ndash; French-Canadian racing driver [[Gilles Villeneuve]] is killed during qualifying for the [[Belgian Grand Prix]].
* [[May 12]]
** Spanish priest [[Juan María Fernández y Krohn]] tries to stab [[Pope John Paul II]] with a [[bayonet]] during the latter's pilgrimage to the shrine at [[Fátima, Portugal|Fátima]].
** [[Braniff International Airways]] is declared bankrupt and ceases all flights.
* [[May 16]] &ndash; The [[New York Islanders]] sweep the [[Vancouver Canucks]] in 4 games to win the [[1982 Stanley Cup Final]].
* [[May 18]] &ndash; [[Falklands War]]: The British [[Special Air Service]] launches an [[Operation Mikado|operation]] to destroy three Argentinean [[Exocet]] [[missile]]s and five [[Dassault-Breguet Super Étendard|Super Étendard]] [[fighter-bomber]]s in mainland [[Argentina]]. It fails when the Argentineans discover about the plot.
* [[May 21]] &ndash; [[Falklands War]]: British landings spark the [[Battle of San Carlos (1982)|Battle of San Carlos]].
* [[May 22]]
** [[Falklands War]]: {{HMS|Ardent|F184|6}} is sunk by Argentine aircraft, killing 22 sailors.
** The [[International Maritime Organization]] (IMO) is established.
* [[May 23]] &ndash; Falklands War: {{HMS|Antelope|F170|6}} is lost.
* [[May 24]]
** [[Iran]]ian troops retake [[Khorramshahr]].
** [[KGB]] head [[Yuri Andropov]] is appointed to the Secretariat of the [[Communist Party of the Soviet Union]].
* [[May 25]] &ndash; British ships {{HMS|Coventry|D118|6}} and {{SS|Atlantic Conveyor}} are sunk during the [[Falklands War]]; Coventry by two [[Douglas A-4 Skyhawk|A-4C Skyhawks]] and the latter sunk by an [[Exocet]].
* [[May 26]]
** [[Aston Villa]] wins the [[UEFA Champions League|European Cup]], beating [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] 1–0 after a 69-minute goal by [[Peter Withe]] in [[Rotterdam]].
** [[Kielder Water]], an artificial lake in [[Northumberland]], is opened.
* [[May 27]]
** [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.]] wins the [[FA Cup]], beating [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] 1–0 in a replay.
** [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] candidate [[Tim Smith (British politician)|Tim Smith]] holds the seat of [[Beaconsfield (UK Parliament constituency)|Beaconsfield]] in a [[Beaconsfield by-election, 1982|by-election]]. The [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] candidate is future [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] [[Tony Blair]].
* [[May 28]] &ndash; [[Pope John Paul II's visit to the United Kingdom]], the first by a reigning pope, begins.
* [[May 28]] &ndash; [[May 29|29]] &ndash; [[Falklands War]]: [[Battle of Goose Green]]: British forces defeat a larger Argentine force.
* [[May 30]]
** Spain becomes the 16th member of [[NATO]] and the first nation to enter the alliance since West Germany's admission in [[1955]].
** [[Indianapolis 500]]: In what [[Indianapolis Motor Speedway]] historian [[Donald Davidson (historian)|Donald Davidson]] and Speedway public address announcer Tom Carnegie later call the greatest moment in the track's history, [[1973]] winner [[Gordon Johncock]] wins his second race over [[1979]] winner [[Rick Mears]] by 0.16 seconds, the closest finish to this date, after Mears draws alongside Johncock with a lap remaining, after erasing a seemingly insurmountable advantage of more than 11 seconds in the final 10 laps.
** [[Hussain Muhammad Ershad]] seizes power in Bangladesh.
** [[Cal Ripken, Jr.]] plays the first of what eventually becomes his record-breaking streak of  2,632 consecutive [[Major League Baseball consecutive games played streaks|Major League Baseball]] games.

=== June ===
* [[June 6]]
** The [[1982 Lebanon War]] begins: Forces under [[Israel]]i Defense Minister [[Ariel Sharon]] invade southern [[Lebanon]] in their "Operation Peace for the Galilee," eventually reaching as far north as the capital [[Beirut]].
** The [[United Nations Security Council]] votes to demand that [[Israel]] withdraw its troops from [[Lebanon]].
* [[June 8]]
** President [[Ronald Reagan]] becomes the first American chief executive to address a joint session of the [[British Parliament]].
** [[Falklands War]]: British ship [[RFA Sir Galahad (1966)|RFA ''Sir Galahad'']] is destroyed during the [[Bluff Cove Air Attacks]]
** [[VASP Flight 168]], a [[Boeing 727]] passenger jet, crashes into forest [[Fortaleza]], killing 137.
* [[June 11]]
** U.S. release date of ''[[E.T.: The Extra-Terrestrial]]'', which would become the biggest box-office hit for the rest of the decade (and beyond).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmsite.org/greatfilmssummary.html|title=Top Films of All-Time|work=filmsite.org}}</ref> 
* [[June 12]] &ndash; The Nuclear Disarmament Rally, an event against [[nuclear weapon]] proliferation, draws 750,000 to New York City's [[Central Park]].  [[Jackson Browne]], [[James Taylor]], [[Bruce Springsteen]], and [[Linda Ronstadt]] attend. An international convocation at The Cathedral of St. John the Divine features prominent peace activists from around the world and afterward participants march on Fifth Avenue to Central Park for the rally.
* [[June 13]]
** The [[1982 FIFA World Cup]] begins in Spain.
** [[Fahd of Saudi Arabia|Fahd]] becomes [[King of Saudi Arabia]] upon the death of his brother, [[King Khalid|Khalid]].
* [[June 14]] &ndash; The [[Falklands War]] ends: [[Argentine surrender in the Falklands War|Formal surrender]] of Argentine forces, and liberation of the [[Falkland Islanders]].
* [[June 18]] &ndash; Argentine military dictator [[Leopoldo Galtieri]] resigns, in the wake of his country's defeat in the [[Falklands War]].
* [[June 19]] &ndash; The body of "God's Banker", [[Roberto Calvi]], chairman of [[Banco Ambrosiano]], is found hanging beneath [[Blackfriars Bridge]] in London.
* [[June 21]] &ndash; [[Prince William, Duke of Cambridge|Prince William]] is born at [[St Mary's Hospital, London|St Mary's Hospital]] in [[Paddington]], West London.
* [[June 24]] &ndash; [[British Airways Flight 9]] suffers a temporary four-engine flameout and damage to the exterior of the plane, after flying through the otherwise undetected [[Volcanic ash|ash plume]] from Indonesia's [[Mount Galunggung]].
* [[June 25]] &ndash; The Institute for Puerto Rican Policy is founded in New York City to research and advocate for Puerto Rican and Latino community issues. In [[2006]], it changes its name to the [[National Institute for Latino Policy]].
* [[June 30]] &ndash; The [[Equal Rights Amendment]] falls short of the 38 states needed to pass; [[Phyllis Schlafly]] and other leaders of the [[Christian right]] take credit for its defeat.

=== July ===
* [[July 2]]
** [[Larry Walters]], a.k.a. Lawn Chair Larry, flies {{convert|16000|ft|m}} above [[Long Beach, California]], in a lawn chair with [[weather balloon]]s attached.
** [[Roy Jenkins]] is elected Leader of the [[Social Democratic Party (UK)|Social Democratic Party]].
* [[July 3]] &ndash; [[ASLEF]] train drivers in the United Kingdom go on strike over hours of work; they return to work on [[July 18]].
* [[July 4]] &ndash; Four [[Iran]]ian diplomats are kidnapped upon [[Israel]]'s invasion of [[Lebanon]].
* [[July 6]] &ndash; A lunar eclipse (umbral duration 236 min and total duration 106 min, the longest of the 20th century) occurs.
* [[July 9]]
** [[Pan Am Flight 759]] ([[Boeing 727]]) crashes in [[Kenner, Louisiana]], killing all 146 on board and 8 on the ground.
** Intruder [[Michael Fagan incident|Michael Fagan]] breaches [[Buckingham Palace]] security as far as into the bedroom of [[Elizabeth II]].
* [[July 11]] &ndash; [[Italy national football team|Italy]] beats [[Germany national football team|West Germany]] 3–1 to win the [[1982 FIFA World Cup]] in Spain.
* [[July 12]] &ndash; [[Checker Motors Corporation]] ceases production of automobiles.
* [[July 15]] &ndash; [[Geoffrey Prime]], a [[GCHQ]] civil servant, is remanded in custody on charges under the [[Official Secrets Act 1911]].
* [[July 16]] &ndash; In New York City, the Reverend [[Sun Myung Moon]] is sentenced to 18 months in prison and fined $25,000 for tax fraud and conspiracy to obstruct justice.
* [[July 19]] &ndash; [[William Whitelaw]], [[Home Secretary]], announces that Michael Trestrail (the Queen's bodyguard) has resigned from the [[Metropolitan Police Service]] over a relationship with a male prostitute.
* [[July 20]] &ndash; The [[Provisional IRA]] detonates 2 bombs in central London, killing 8 soldiers, wounding 47 people, and leading to the deaths of 7 horses.
* [[July 21]] &ndash; {{HMS|Hermes|R12|6}}, the [[Royal Navy]] [[flagship]] during the [[Falklands War]], returns home to [[Portsmouth]] to a hero's welcome.
* [[July 23]]
** The [[International Whaling Commission]] decides to end commercial [[whaling]] by 1985–[[1986]].
** A coroner's jury returns a verdict of suicide on [[Roberto Calvi]], who was found hanging under [[Blackfriars Bridge]].
** Torrential rain and mudslides in [[Nagasaki]], Japan destroy bridges and kill 299.
** On a movie set, the ''Twilight Zone'' actor [[Vic Morrow]] and 2 child actors die in a helicopter stunt accident.
* [[July 31]] &ndash; In [[Beaune]], France, 53 persons, 46 of them children, die in a highway accident (France's worst).

=== August ===
* [[August 1]] &ndash; Attempted coup against government of Daniel Arap Moi in [[Kenya]].
* [[August 3]] &ndash; [[Venezuela]] recognizes the [[Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic]] (SADR).
* [[August 4]] &ndash; The [[United Nations Security Council]] votes to [[censure]] Israel because its troops are still in Lebanon.
* [[August 7]] &ndash; Italian Prime Minister [[Giovanni Spadolini]] resigns.
* [[August 11]] &ndash; [[Suriname]] recognizes the [[Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic]] (SADR).
* [[August 12]] &ndash; Mexico announces it is unable to pay its large foreign debt, triggering a [[Latin American debt crisis|debt crisis]] that quickly spread throughout Latin America.
* [[August 13]] &ndash; In Hong Kong, health warnings on cigarette packets are made statutory.<ref>Clarence Tsui, ''[[South China Morning Post]]'', 12/12/99, "[http://lists.essential.org/intl-tobacco/msg00330.html From ads to ashes]." ([http://www.webcitation.org/6BIDnnMnh Archive])</ref>
* [[August 17]] &ndash; The first [[compact disc]]s (CDs) are released to the public in Germany.
* [[August 20]] &ndash; [[Lebanese Civil War]]: A multinational force lands in [[Beirut]] to oversee the [[PLO]] withdrawal from [[Lebanon]]. French troops arrive [[August 21]], U.S. Marines [[August 25]].

=== September ===
* [[September 3]] &ndash; Italian general [[Carlo Alberto Dalla Chiesa]] is killed in a Mafia ambush.
* [[September 5]] &ndash; [[Iowa]] paperboy [[Johnny Gosch]] is kidnapped.
* [[September 14]] &ndash; [[Lebanon|Lebanese]] President-elect [[Bachir Gemayel]] is assassinated in [[Beirut]].
* [[September 18]] &ndash; The Lebanese Christian militia (the [[Kataeb|Phalange]]) [[Sabra and Shatila massacre|kill thousands of Palestinians]] in the Sabra and Shatila refugee camps with the overlooking of Israeli troops in West Beirut. The massacre is in retaliation for the assassination of pro-Israel president-elect, Bachir Gemayel, as well as several Palestinian massacres against Lebanese Christians.
* [[September 19]] &ndash; The first [[emoticon]]s are posted by [[Scott Fahlman]].
* [[September 21]] &ndash; The first [[International Day of Peace]] is proclaimed by the  ([[United Nations]]).
* [[September 23]] &ndash; [[Amine Gemayel]], brother of Bachir, is elected president of [[Lebanon]].
* [[September 24]] &ndash; The [[Wimpy Operation]], first act of armed resistance against Israeli troops in [[Beirut]].
* [[September 25]] &ndash; In Israel, 400,000 marchers demand the resignation of Prime Minister [[Menachem Begin]].
* [[September 26]] &ndash; [[Thermal]]s take Australian parachutist Rich Collins up to {{convert|2800|m|ft}} during a jump; he almost blacks out due to lack of oxygen. He releases his main [[parachute]] to fall to lower altitude and lands by his reserve parachute.
* [[September 29]] &ndash; [[October 1]] &ndash; The [[Chicago Tylenol murders]] occur when 7 people in the Chicago area die after ingesting capsules laced with [[potassium cyanide]].

=== October ===
* [[October 1]]
** [[Helmut Kohl]] replaces [[Helmut Schmidt]] as [[Chancellor of Germany (Federal Republic)|Chancellor of Germany]] through a [[constructive vote of no confidence]].
** In [[Orlando, Florida]], [[Walt Disney World]] opens the second largest theme park, [[Epcot|EPCOT Center]], to the public for the first time.
** [[Sony]] launches the first consumer [[compact disc]] (CD) player (model [[Sony CDP-101|CDP-101]]).
* [[October 4]]
** [[Glenn Gould]], Canadian pianist, dies from a stroke. Later, his recently re-recorded "Aria" from the [[Goldberg Variations]],  by [[J.S. Bach]], is played at the end of his funeral service. 
* [[October 8]] 
** Poland bans [[Solidarity (Polish trade union)|Solidarity]] after having suspended it on 13 December 1981.
** After six years in opposition, Social Democrat [[Olof Palme]] becomes once again [[Prime Minister of Sweden]] .
* [[October 11]] &ndash; The ''[[Mary Rose]]'', flagship of [[Henry VIII of England]] that sank in [[1545]], is raised from the [[Solent]].
* [[October 12]] &ndash; [[Thorbjörn Fälldin]] returns as [[Prime Minister of Sweden]] .
* [[October 13]] &ndash; The [[Ford Sierra]] is launched in Europe, replacing the [[Ford Cortina]] (which was known as the [[Ford Taunus]] on continental Europe).
* [[October 19]] &ndash; [[John DeLorean]] is arrested for selling [[cocaine]] to undercover [[FBI]] agents (he is later found not guilty due to [[entrapment]]).
* [[October 20]]
** [[Luzhniki disaster]]: During the [[UEFA Cup]] match between [[FC Spartak Moscow]] and [[HFC Haarlem]], 66 people are crushed to death.
* [[October 27]]
** In Canada, [[Dominion Day]] is officially renamed [[Canada Day]].
** The [[Homosexual Offences (Northern Ireland) Order 1982]] comes into effect, decriminalising homosexuality in Northern Ireland for those aged 18 or older.
* [[October 28]] &ndash; The [[Spanish Socialist Workers' Party|Socialist Party]] wins the [[Spanish general election, 1982|election]] in Spain; [[Felipe González]] is elected Prime Minister.

=== November ===
* [[November 2]] &ndash; [[Channel 4]], a British public-service television broadcaster, is launched.
* [[November 3]]
** A gasoline or petrol tanker explodes in the [[Salang Tunnel]] in [[Afghanistan]], killing at least 176 people.
** The [[Dow Jones Industrial Average]] surges 43.41 points, or 4.25%, to close at 1,065.49, its first all-time high in more than 9 years. It last hit a record on January 11, 1973 when the average closed at 1,051.70. The points gain is the biggest ever up to this point.
* [[November 6]] &ndash; [[Cameroon]] president [[Ahmadou Ahidjo]] resigns, replaced by [[Paul Biya]].
* [[November 7]] &ndash; The [[Thames Barrier]] is first publicly demonstrated.
* [[November 8]] &ndash; [[Kenan Evren]] becomes the seventh president of [[Turkey]] as a result of constitution referendum. His former title was '''head of state'''.
* [[November 12]] &ndash; In the [[Soviet Union]], former [[KGB]] head [[Yuri Andropov]] is selected to become the general secretary of the Soviet Communist Party's Central Committee, succeeding the late [[Leonid Brezhnev|Leonid I. Brezhnev]].
* [[November 13]] &ndash; The [[Vietnam Veterans Memorial]] is dedicated in Washington, D.C., after a march to its site by thousands of [[Vietnam War]] veterans.
* [[November 14]] &ndash; The leader of Poland's outlawed [[Solidarity (Polish trade union)|Solidarity]] movement, [[Lech Wałęsa]], is released from 11 months of internment near the [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] border.
* [[November 20]]
**The [[General Union of Ecuadorian Workers]] (UGTE) is founded.
**[[University of California, Berkeley]] executes "[[The Play (Stanford vs. California)|The Play]]" in a [[college football]] game against [[Stanford University|Stanford]].  Completing a wacky 57-yard kickoff return that includes 5laterals, Kevin Moen runs through Stanford band members who had prematurely come onto the field. His touchdown stands and California wins 25–20.
* [[November 25]] &ndash; The [[Minneapolis Thanksgiving Day fire]] destroys an entire city block of downtown Minneapolis, including the headquarters of [[Northwestern National Bank]].
* [[November 27]] &ndash; [[Yasuhiro Nakasone]] becomes Prime Minister of Japan.
* [[November 28]] 
** Representatives from 88 countries gather in [[Geneva]] to discuss [[International trade|world trade]] and ways to work toward aspects of [[free trade]].
** The [[Edmonton Eskimos]] win an unprecedented 5th [[consecutive]] [[Grey Cup]]; a feat yet unaccomplished by any professional football franchise to win the [[70th Grey Cup]] defeating the [[Toronto Argonauts]] 32-16.<ref>[http://www.cfl.ca/page/his_greycup_recap1982]</ref>
* [[November 30]] &ndash; [[Michael Jackson]] releases ''[[Thriller (Michael Jackson album)|Thriller]]'', the biggest selling [[album]] of all time.

=== December ===
* [[December 1]] &ndash; [[Miguel de la Madrid]] takes office as [[President of Mexico]].
* [[December 2]] &ndash; At the [[University of Utah]], 61-year-old retired dentist [[Barney Clark (patient)|Barney Clark]] becomes the first person to receive a permanent [[artificial heart]] (he lives for 112 days with the device).
* [[December 3]] &ndash; A final soil sample is taken from the site of [[Times Beach, Missouri]].   It is found to contain 300 times the safe level of [[Polychlorinated dibenzodioxins|dioxin]].
* [[December 4]] &ndash; The People's Republic of China adopts its [[constitution of the People's Republic of China|current constitution]].
* [[December 7]] &ndash; The first U.S. [[capital punishment|execution]] by [[lethal injection]] is carried out in [[Texas]].
* [[December 8]] &ndash; The [[December murders]] occur in Suriname.
* [[December 11]] &ndash; Pop group [[ABBA]] make their final public performance on the British TV programme ''[[The Late, Late Breakfast Show]]''.
* [[December 12]] &ndash; [[Greenham Common Women's Peace Camp|Women's peace protest at RAF Greenham Common]]: 30,000 women hold hands and form a human chain around the 14.5&nbsp;km (9&nbsp;mi) perimeter fence.
* [[December 13]] &ndash; The 6.0 [[Surface wave magnitude|Ms]] [[1982 North Yemen earthquake|North Yemen earthquake]] shakes southwestern [[Yemen]] with a maximum [[Mercalli intensity scale|Mercalli intensity]] of VIII (''Severe''), killing 2,800.
* [[December 14]] &ndash; [[Bolivia]] recognizes the [[Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic]] (SADR).
* [[December 16]] &ndash; The [[United Freedom Front]] bombs an office of [[South African Airways]] in Elmont, NY and an [[IBM]] office in Harrison, NY.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1982/12/17/nyregion/bombs-rock-ibm-building-and-airline-office.html|title=Bombs Rock I.B.M. Building And Airline Office|date=December 17, 1982|website=nytimes.com}}</ref> Two police officers suffer hearing damage. The UFF claimed responsibility for the IBM building bombing in March 1984, stating that the company was targeted because of its business in South Africa under [[Apartheid]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=iREhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=KHQFAAAAIBAJ&pg=3707%2C1069528|title=Schenectady Gazette &ndash; Google News Archive Search|work=google.com}}</ref>
